> Well, it seems that someone has patented some new forms of punctuation: [WIPO Patent Publication No. WO9219458](http://v3.espacenet.com/textdoc?DB=EPODOC&IDX=WO9219458&F=0):
> > Using two new punctuation marks, the question comma and the exclamation comma: and respectively, inquisitiveness and exclamation may be expressed within a written sentence structure, so that thoughts may be more easily and clearly conveyed to readers. The new punctuation marks are for use within a written sentence between words as a comma, but with more feeling or inquisitiveness.Seems that this is sort of an addition to the faddish punctuation known as the [Interrobang](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interrobang).
