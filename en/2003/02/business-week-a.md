I did an interview with Irene a couple of months ago about the government's idea about bailing out small businesses. I [blogged about how throwing it around or letting so called "experts" doesn't make sense](http://joi.ito.com/archives/2002/10/24/nbc_press_conference.html). Having said that, we received funding from a government backed fund which is managed by professionals. Singapore also has a variety of well manged government funds. If the government is going to put money into the market, choosing the right people to run the fund is essential. The "old way" just greases the political machine. The difficulty is choosing the people who choose the companies and make the investments. Transparency is probably a good place to start. Incentives are also important. The devil is in the details and it's quite difficult.
