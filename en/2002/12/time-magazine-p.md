[The whistleblowers at WorldCom, Enron and the FBI, Cynthia Cooper, Sherron Watkins and Colleen Rowley won the honor from Time Magazine this year.](http://www.time.com/time/personoftheyear/) This is great. They deserve it. They must be protected and honored.

You can't have whistleblowers without privacy and the [whistleblower protection bill that I am working on](http://joi.ito.com/archives/2002/11/05/protecting_whistleblowers.html) is also an important component. I've missed the last few meetings and I feel guilty. I see the bill headed towards bureaucratic mumbo-jumbo that won't help anyone. *Mental note to myself to make comments on the current draft which I don't agree with.* I wonder if the day will come when Japan honors whistleblowers like this. Time says, " They took huge professional and personal risks to blow the whistle on what went wrong at WorldCom, Enron and the FBIâ€”and in so doing helped remind us what American courage and American values are all about." I hope that some day I can say this about Japanese values. Today, I don't think I can.
